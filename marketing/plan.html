30-Day Social Media Growth & Conversion Plan
TikTok + Instagram organic strategy, content calendar, and UI commercial brief
Prepared for the founder — athlete-led launch

1. The Goal — and an Honest Reframe

Stated goal: 1,000 people, then 6,000 people, within 30 days. Whether that's realistic depends entirely on whether “people” means free signups or paid subscribers.

Metric30-day feasibilityWhy
6,000 free signups (Other tier + browsers)AchievableOne athlete-POV TikTok that hits can reach 500K+ views.
6,000 paid subscribers ($5.99/mo)Extremely unlikelyThat is ~$36K MRR from a standing start — a rare outlier for any bootstrapped product in month one.

The winning approach is a funnel, not a single number:

  1. Acquire thousands of FREE signups (the Other tier) — they cost nothing and generate content + social proof.
  2. Get athletes to leave reviews — this is your content moat and the reason the next person subscribes.
  3. Convert a slice (realistically 2–5%) to paid once they hit the “I need to post my own review” moment.

The free Other tier is the top of this funnel. Lead with free; upsell to paid.

2. Your Unfair Advantage: An Athlete With a Network

Do not start with cold ads. Start warm. Seed the platform with real reviews from athletes you personally know so it isn't a ghost town when strangers arrive.

Week 0 — Warm Seed (before any public content)

  • DM 30–50 athletes you know: teammates, opponents, friends on other teams.
  • Ask each to sign up and leave ONE honest review of a coach, program, or dorm they've experienced.
  • Target: 50–100 reviews live BEFORE you post a single TikTok.

This is the single most important step. An empty review site converts no one.

4. The UI Commercial — Shot-by-Shot Brief

Total length: 20–25 seconds (TikTok / Reels sweet spot). Produce in CapCut (free) using screen recordings of the live site. Vertical 9:16, captions always on.

Primary Cut: “The Honest Athlete”

Production Notes (all free tools)

  • Screen recording: iPhone built-in recorder on the live site, or QuickTime on Mac. Record slow, deliberate scrolls; speed up 1.5–2x in editing.
  • Editing: CapCut (free). Use auto-captions; layer a rising trending sound at low volume under your voiceover.
  • The hook is everything: the first 2 seconds decide whether the video lives or dies. Make 5 hook variants for the same body footage and test.
  • Vertical 9:16, captions on (80% of viewers watch muted).
  • Trending audio: pick a sound that's rising, not peaked — check TikTok Creative Center.

Make 3 Variants to Test

  1. “The Honest Athlete” — utility / problem angle (above).
  2. “Coaches don't want this” — controversy angle, same footage, spicier hook.
  3. “I built this” — founder story, more you on camera, less UI.

Post all 3 across a week. Double down on whichever earns the most watch-time + saves.

5. Conversion Mechanics — Viewers to Subscribers

  1. Link in bio points straight to /pricing (already the entry point).
  2. The free Other tier removes the signup wall: people join free, browse, get hooked, then hit “I want to post MY review” → paywall → subscribe.
  3. Referral loop: end every post with “tag a teammate who needs to see their coach's reviews.”
  4. Reviews ARE the demo: every juicy anonymized review you post is itself an ad.
